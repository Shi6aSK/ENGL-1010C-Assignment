Introduction to Quantum Computing

Quantum Bits (Qubits)

Unlike classical bits that can be either 0 or 1, qubits can exist in multiple states simultaneously through superposition.

Superposition

The ability of quantum systems to exist in multiple states at once, enabling parallel processing capabilities.

Entanglement

A quantum phenomenon where particles become correlated in ways that classical physics cannot explain.

Why Quantum Computing?

Unprecedented Processing Power

Solve complex problems in hours that would take classical computers millions of years.

Cryptography Revolution

Transform data security with quantum encryption methods.

Scientific Breakthroughs

Enable molecular simulations for drug discovery and materials science.
